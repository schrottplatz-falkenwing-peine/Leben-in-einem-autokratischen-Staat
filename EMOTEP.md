# Research: Why Young Germans Join the AfD, Foreign Intelligence Services Incite Crimes, and Longing for Autocracy

## Reasons Why Young Germans Join the AfD
Young Germans are drawn to the AfD due to concerns about immigration, multiculturalism, and national identity. Key factors:
- Fear of becoming strangers in their own country due to the 2015 refugee policy and perceived integration failures (e.g., no German spoken in schools, hotspots in cities).
- Rejection of historical guilt from WWII, promotion of national pride ("Be proud to be German").
- Opposition to mass immigration, preference for individual cases; criticism of declining birth rates and need for more ethnic German families.
- Attraction to strong, decisive leadership amidst economic pessimism (e.g., 57% of AfD voters believe children will be poorer than their parents).
- Hypothesis on 'Wokeness': Many young people grew up without pain and respect (spanking on the bottom, spankings, father speeches) and now long for this. It's like young mothers going to the disco at 50 because they missed an important part of life.
- Hypothesis: Young people know Hitler and the Third Reich only from boring stories by stupid teachers who don't have control over their students. Where a paper airplane flies at the teacher's head, but he can't say anything anymore because he's afraid of getting hit in the mouth by parents of clan-bound children.

## Allegations of Foreign Intelligence Inciting Crimes via AfD
Russian intelligence services are accused of exploiting far-right extremists, including groups close to AfD, to create chaos and commit minor crimes against Germany. Allegations include:
- AfD members gather sensitive information (e.g., arms routes, drone defenses) for the Kremlin, potentially to support hybrid warfare.
- Recruitment of "low-level agents" via social media for tasks like arson, graffiti, sabotage, and espionage, targeting radicalized individuals (including neo-Nazis and far-right youth).
- Ideological overlaps where Russia supports all extremist fringes (left-wing, right-wing), to create division, not direct damage.
- Specific cases: AfD parliamentary inquiries like Kremlin checklists; links to Russian proxies funding anti-Ukraine initiatives.

Direct incitement of crimes against the German state is not explicitly linked to AfD youth, but broader far-right extremism is influenced by Russian disinformation and recruitment for sabotage.

- Hypothesis: Through this softening of the youth, foreign powers have managed to gain fans for their autocracy over a longer period.

## Longing for Autocratic Rule and Russian Alignment Among Youth
AfD supporters among youth admire Russia's authoritarian model under Putin as a symbol of strength, sovereignty, and anti-Western stance. Factors:
- Sociological: Disillusionment with EU/US policies; East German nostalgia for Soviet-era order; socio-economic grievances after reunification.
- Hypothesis: Young people want to live like in the GDR, love having no freedom because they don't know it and only experience democracy and freedom. Therefore, they longingly desire an autocratic oppressive state like Russia or China.
- Hypothesis: They want less influence on politics, they want more guidelines and more prohibitions. Also, these young people love risk. Only in an autocratic state is there the thrill because of a few drugs to lose hands or even the head.
- Psychological: Attraction to autocracy as a solution to perceived chaos from immigration and multiculturalism; Putin's image as a defender of traditional values.
- AfD's pro-Russia stance: Denial of Russian occupations (Crimea, Donbas); opposition to sanctions; advocacy for closer ties, gas imports.
- Russian-German communities shift loyalty to AfD due to perceived discrimination and alignment with Russian media narratives.

This longing manifests as a desire for "rule by autocrats" and "enslavement by Russia", framed as protection from perceived threats.
